Background of UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

The UN General Assembly adopted the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development in 2015, this new global framework aims to redirect humanity towards a sustainable path to engage a better future for human well-being. The UN General Assembly adopted the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development in 2015, this new global framework aims to redirect humanity towards a sustainable path to engage a better future for human well-being.

At the core of the 2030 Agenda are 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), 169 specific targets and 230 reference indicators, addressing a range of social needs including education, health, social protection and job opportunities while tackling climate change and environmental protection. Thus, with respect to the SDGs, all countries can be considered as developing and need to take urgent action to achieve sustainability. As it can be seen from the SDG4 (Quality Education), education for sustainable development is also an important milestone in achieving sustainable development. The United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) has promoted the integration of sustainable development education into teaching and learning.

Aims of this website

Under the vision for the implementation of SDG 11, it is urgent for people to cultivate competency for evaluate and understand environmental changes and sustainable city and community planning, so as to establish a sense of sustainable development to promote self reflection and attendance to related planning. Therefore, this website hopes to provide students with a digital learning environment, with several functions developed to explore the land cover changes and urban growth characteristics of Kaohsiung City from 1985 to 2015, and provide relevant assessments that can stimulate independent thinking.
